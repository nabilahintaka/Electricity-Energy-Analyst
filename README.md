# ⚡ Electricity & CO₂ Emissions Analysis

## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes global electricity production data to uncover patterns across **energy sources, CO₂ emissions, economic indicators, and energy accessibility.** The analysis combines **data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and visualization** to generate actionable insights for understanding global energy trends and supporting sustainable decision-making.

---

## 📌 Summary

This analysis reveals that electricity production patterns are strongly linked to **CO₂ emissions and economic development.** Countries heavily dependent on fossil fuels tend to produce significantly higher emissions, while renewable energy adoption is increasing but remains uneven across regions.  

The findings show that **economic strength and energy access are closely connected**, highlighting that a balanced transition strategy is needed to achieve sustainable energy goals globally.

---

## 🔄 Data Processing

* Cleaned and standardized column names (snake_case)
* Handled missing values and removed incomplete records
* Converted data types for accurate analysis
* Validated dataset consistency (no duplicate data found)

---

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis

* Analyzed electricity production across:
  - Fossil fuels  
  - Nuclear  
  - Renewable energy  
* Identified top countries contributing to CO₂ emissions  
* Explored the relationship between:
  - GDP per capita and electricity access  
* Compared energy patterns between developed and developing countries  

---

## 🎯 Key Insights

* ⚡ Fossil fuel dependency drives higher CO₂ emissions  
* 🌱 Renewable energy adoption is growing but not yet dominant  
* 🌍 Developed countries have better electricity access and cleaner energy usage  
* 📊 GDP per capita positively influences electricity accessibility
